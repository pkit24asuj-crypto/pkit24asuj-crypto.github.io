Overview

Nepal, officially the Federal Democratic Republic of Nepal, is a landlocked country in South Asia. It is mainly situated in the Himalayas, but also includes parts of the Indo-Gangetic Plain. Nepal borders the Tibet Autonomous Region of China to the north, and India in the south, east, and west, while it is narrowly separated from Bangladesh by the Siliguri Corridor, and from Bhutan by the Indian state of Sikkim.

Nepal has a diverse geography, including fertile plains, subalpine forested hills, and eight of the world's ten tallest mountains, including Mount Everest, the highest point on Earth. Kathmandu is the nation's capital and the largest city. Nepal is a multiethnic, multiracial, multicultural, multireligious, and multilingual country.

Geography

Nepal is located between China's Tibet Autonomous Region to the north and India to the south, east and west. The country occupies 147,516 km² (56,956 sq mi), making it roughly the size of Bangladesh or the U.S. state of Arkansas.

Topography

Nepal can be divided into three main geographical regions:

  • The Terai: A lowland region in southern Nepal
  • The Hill Region: The central belt of Nepal
  • The Mountain Region: The northern region containing the Himalayas

History

Nepal's history dates back to ancient times. The country has a rich cultural heritage influenced by both Hindu and Buddhist traditions.

Ancient Period

The earliest known civilization in Nepal was the Kirat dynasty, which ruled from the 7th century BC. The Licchavi period (400-750 AD) saw the development of art, architecture, and culture.

Medieval Period

The Malla period (1200-1769 AD) was marked by the flourishing of arts, architecture, and trade. During this time, the Kathmandu Valley was divided into three kingdoms: Kathmandu, Patan, and Bhaktapur.

Modern Nepal

In 1768, Prithvi Narayan Shah unified Nepal and established the Shah dynasty. Nepal became a federal democratic republic in 2008, ending centuries of monarchical rule.

Culture and Society

Nepal is incredibly diverse, with over 100 ethnic groups and more than 90 languages spoken throughout the country.

Religion

Nepal is the birthplace of Lord Buddha and home to numerous Hindu temples and Buddhist monasteries. The majority of the population practices Hinduism (81.3%), followed by Buddhism (9.0%).

Festivals

Nepal celebrates numerous festivals throughout the year, including:

  • Dashain - The longest and most significant festival
  • Tihar - Festival of lights
  • Holi - Festival of colors
  • Buddha Jayanti - Buddha's birthday

Economy

Nepal's economy is primarily based on agriculture, which employs about 65% of the population. The country also relies heavily on remittances from Nepali workers abroad.

Key Sectors

  • Agriculture: Rice, wheat, maize, and cash crops
  • Tourism: Trekking, mountaineering, and cultural tourism
  • Hydropower: Significant potential for electricity generation
  • Manufacturing: Textiles, carpets, and handicrafts
